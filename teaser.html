Every Robotaxi Company In The World Skipped India.

The global robotaxi race has absorbed $21B+ — Waymo, Zoox, Wayve, Momenta, Pony.ai — and now runs in the US, Europe, and China: every major auto market but one. India, the world's 3rd-largest auto market with 3M+ daily ride-hailing trips, has zero autonomous players. Navixar exists to be the first. Here's the problem, the plan, and why both the tech and the money work.

03 · Why It's Technically Possible

The Hard Parts Have Been
De-Risked — Elsewhere.

✓

The algorithms are no longer the moat

Foundation driving policies, VLA architectures, and open-source stacks are converging fast — Momenta, Wayve, and Pony have proven stack-building is a known engineering art. What's scarce is Indian data and Indian validation. That's exactly what we build.

✓

Country-scale data without a fleet

Waymo's playbook costs ~$20M+ and 3–5 years per city. Drones, dashcam acquisition, and gamified crowdsourcing get India-diverse data for under $1M, in months — riding a billion smartphones and the world's cheapest mobile data.

✓

Testing works like a flight simulator — and the tech is proven

Pilots train in simulators before touching a real plane; our AI does the same. Film a real Indian street, rebuild it as a 3D copy so faithful you can re-drive it — every pothole, every glare — then test the AI there a million times, safely. The technique behind this (Gaussian splatting) is now industry standard, backed by NVIDIA and Cesium — we simply apply it to the one geography nobody has rebuilt. And the founder has spent 7 years building data & simulation pipelines for India's own OEMs.

04 · Why The Economics Work

The Money Works Before
The First Robotaxi Rolls.

₹

80% of every fare is the prize

Under India's MVAG 2025 rules, at least 80% of every ride-hailing fare goes to the driver — who pays fuel, EMI, and maintenance out of it. Autonomy replaces that entire human cost stack with machine economics that get cheaper every year. Wages only rise.

₹

Revenue starts years before robotaxis

India is short 2.2 million truck drivers — 25–30% of fleets sit idle. Autonomous port & logistics yards (private land, no public-road approval needed) buy now. Then OEM licensing: royalties per vehicle on India's 4M+ annual car sales.

₹

The model is already priced — at $9B

Momenta IPO'd in Hong Kong at a $9B valuation in July 2026 on exactly this play: license the driving stack to OEMs, use the margin to fund robotaxis. Wayve holds $8.5B pre-revenue. Every major market prices in one AV champion. India's seat is empty.
